## Summary of Climate Change Foundations (Wikipedia)

- Climate change refers to long‑term shifts in temperature and weather patterns, primarily driven by human activities such as greenhouse‑gas emissions from fossil‑fuel combustion.
- The scientific consensus (IPCC) identifies anthropogenic forcing as the dominant cause of warming since the mid‑20th century.
- Observed impacts include rising global temperatures (~1.1 °C above pre‑industrial), sea‑level rise, ocean acidification, loss of ice, and increased frequency of extreme weather events.
- Mitigation focuses on reducing emissions (renewables, efficiency, carbon pricing); adaptation involves building resilient infrastructure.

## Latest NASA Climate Findings (climate.nasa.gov)

- NASA operates >20 Earth‑observing satellites that monitor key climate indicators:
  * **Carbon Dioxide** – continuous atmospheric CO₂ measurements show a steady increase.
  * **Global Temperature** – GISTEMP surface temperature record tracks ongoing warming.
  * **Methane** – rising concentrations reported.
  * **Arctic Sea‑Ice Minimum Extent** – declining trend over decades.
  * **Ice Sheets** – mass loss from Greenland and Antarctica.
  * **Sea Level** – global sea‑level rise documented via satellite altimetry.
  * **Ocean Warming** – increasing heat content in the upper ocean.
- NASA research highlights the link between warming and amplified wildfires, extreme heat, and impacts on ecosystems.
- Interactive tools and data portals provide real‑time visualizations of these indicators for scientists and the public.

These resources provide a solid scientific baseline for the Climate Research Hub project.
